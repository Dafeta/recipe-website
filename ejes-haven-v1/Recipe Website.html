Jollof Rice Recipe

Jollof rice is a dish with deep roots in West African culture, and itâ€™s one that carries with it a sense of community, celebration, and tradition.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Rice
  • 1 tbsp Tomato Paste
  • Fresh tomatoes and Fresh Pepper
  • 3 Onions
  • 3 Bay Leaf
  • 1 red bell pepper aka Tatashe
  • 1/2 cup Veg Oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 stock cube
  • Thyme and Curry

Preparations

  1. Pour warm or hot water over the rice and allow it soak for 30 minutes.
  2. Wash and blend, tomatoes, tatashe, 2 onions, pepper, garlic and ginger, then par boil till water dries out.
  3. dice the remaining onion and set aside.
  4. Heat up pot and add veg oil and then onions, saute for a minute then add tomatoes paste
  5. Add bay leaf to get the flavour infused into the oil; add the parboiled tomatoes paste. Cook for about 7 to 8 minutes, Stir. Add salt and stock cube once the paste is cooked through
  6. Finally add your washed rice and water; turn to mix everything together and then cover the pot with foil paper before closing the lid; cook on medium heat
  7. Check regularly to make sure you dont't need extra water, taste so you can adjust the ingredients.

Edikaikong (Edikang Ikong Soup)

Edikaikong soup is the real vegetable soup of the Ibibios or Efiks people of South South region in Nigeria. The soup involves a lots of preparation and honestly, requires me to be a very good mood to get the consistency right.

Reviews 345

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs Malabar Spinach aka Water leaf
  • 2 lbs Pumpkin leaves or Ugwu
  • 3 lbs goat meat cut into large bite size cubes
  • 1/2 cup of Cooked shelled Apple snails
  • 1 lbs Smoked Shrimp
  • 1/2 cup of Palm oil
  • 2 Red onions
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers
  • 4 tbsp Ground smoked dried shrimp aka Crayfish
  • 3 tsp Chicken bouillion
  • Salt to taste

Preparations

  1. Slice both onions and scotch bonnet peppers, set aside.
  2. On medium heat in a large stock pot, braise the goat meat with one of the onions, scotch bonnet peppers, 1 tsp bouillion and 1tsp of salt until the meat is tender.
    Half way into braising the goat meat, add 1/2 cup of water and stir the meat to prevent burning. Keep the pot covered during the braising process.
  3. While the meat is braising, wash the Malabar Spinach and Pumpkin leaves (separately) in cool water to get rid of sand and dirt. Pick off the tough stems, save the tender stems and leaves. Chop and set aside.
  4. Once the meat is done braising, Set aside.
  5. On medium heat, Heat up the palm oil in a deep pot. Add the other sliced onion, cook for 10 minutes, until the onion looks melted.
  6. Add the snails and smoked shrimp, continue to cook for another 5 minutes.
  7. Add in the braised goat meat (without the broth).
  8. Add 2 tsp of bouillion, crayfish, the chopped water leaves and pumpkin leaves. Stir and allow to cook for 10 minutes under medium heat.
  9. After 10 minutes, tatse the seasoning and adjust the salt if necessary.
  10. Serve hot.
